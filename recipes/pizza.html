Pizza

A classic homemade pizza features a customizable crust, fresh sauce, and a variety of toppings, offering a delicious and satisfying meal. This recipe provides a foundational approach to making a versatile pizza from scratch.

Ingredients

  • Warm Water (2 cups)
  • Active Dry Yeast (2 teaspoons)
  • Granulated White Sugar (2 tablespoons)
  • Extra-Virgin Olive Oil (2 tablespoons)
  • All-Purpose Flour (4 cups)
  • Salt (2 teaspoons)
  • Pizza Sauce (8 oz)
  • Mozarella Chesse (8 oz)
  • Monterey Jack Cheese (6 oz)
  • Pepperoni (optional)
  • Spinach Leaves (1 cup, optional)
  • Garlic Butter (as desired, optional)

Steps

  1. In a large mixing bowl, pour in the warm water.
  2. Add the active dry yeast and sugar; let sit for 5 minutes until the mixture is frothy.
  3. Pour in the olive oil and gently stir with a spatula until combined.
  4. Add in the flour and salt; mix with the spatula until a slightly sticky ball forms. Create a dough ball (using a bit more flour if needed).
  5. Transfer your dough ball to a floured surface and knead until the dough is smooth.
  6. Split the dough in half and roll them into your desired shape. Transfer the dough to two sheet pans sprayed with non-stick cooking spray.
  7. Poke holes in the dough with a fork to avoid the crusts puffing up. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 425Â°F.
  8. Parbake the crusts for about 5 minutes.
  9. Remove the par-baked crusts and spread tomato sauce on each. Add your cheese, meats, and veggies on top.
  10. Bake for 10 - 13 minutes, until the crust is golden brown and cheese is fully melted.
  11. Remove from the oven and brush the crusts with garlic butter.
  12. Top with your favorite pizza toppings: crushed red pepper, parmesan flakes, grated cheese, balsamic glaze, etc. Enjoy!
